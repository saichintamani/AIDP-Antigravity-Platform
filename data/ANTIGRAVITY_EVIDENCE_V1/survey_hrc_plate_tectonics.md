## 1. Available Evidence

### EV_PT_STATIC (Publication)
> The continents are fixed in place. The Earth's crust moves primarily vertically due to thermal contraction and geosynclinal development.


### EV_PT_RIDGES (Publication)
> Sonar mapping reveals massive mid-ocean ridges stretching across the globe. High heat flow is measured at the crest of these ridges.


### EV_PT_MAGNETIC (Publication)
> Oceanographic surveys show strange, zebra-like patterns of magnetic anomalies (alternating high and low magnetic intensities) on the seafloor parallel to the mid-ocean ridges.


### EV_PT_WAGENER (Publication)
> Alfred Wegener's 'continental drift' hypothesis is widely rejected because there is no known physical mechanism that could force solid continents through the solid oceanic crust.


### Explicit Scientific Constraints
- Magnetic anomaly stripes are symmetric about the mid-ocean ridge axis
- Heat flow is highest at ridge crests and decreases with distance
- No known mechanism can force solid continents through solid oceanic crust
- Continental coastlines (Africa, South America) show geometric fit
