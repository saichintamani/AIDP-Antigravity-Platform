## The Evidentiary Trajectory

The project has transitioned from building the instrument to building a body of evidence. The future of Antigravity is dictated by the following observation pipeline:

### Phase A — Observation
*   **Question**: *What happens?*
*   **Action**: Collect reproductions, failures, and contradictions. Do not explain them away.

### Phase B — Explanation
*   **Question**: *Why does it happen?*
*   **Action**: Only after enough observations exist, allow hypotheses (H1, H2, H3, H4, H5) to compete.

### Phase C — Survival
*   **Question**: *Which explanations survive scrutiny?*
*   **Action**: Ruthlessly prune failed hypotheses.

### Phase D — Adoption
*   **Question**: *Does anyone use the instrument when the creators are not present?*
*   **Action**: Achieve institutionalized scientific status.

## Intellectual Honesty

One reproduction is an anecdote. Five reproductions are a pattern. Twenty reproductions are evidence. 

The same applies to failures. One failed reproduction does not kill the benchmark. One successful reproduction does not validate it. The objective is accumulation, not confirmation.
