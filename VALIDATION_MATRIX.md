# Validation Matrix

The core objective of Antigravity is not to assert that our internal findings are absolute truth, but to determine whether the temporal leakage phenomenon survives contact with independent, adversarial, and real-world reality.

Every major claim made by the Antigravity project must pass through four scientific gates. 

## The Four Gates

### Gate 1 — Internal Observation
*   **Definition**: Observed by the project creators on local hardware.
*   **Evidence Level**: Weakest acceptable evidence. High risk of self-confirmation bias.
*   **Status**: ✅ Completed for V1 release.

### Gate 2 — Independent Reproduction
*   **Definition**: Observed by someone with a different machine, different environment, and different incentives, with no prior project involvement.
*   **Evidence Level**: Strong.
*   **Target**: Minimum 5 successful reproductions.
*   **Status**: 0% Proven.

### Gate 3 — Adversarial Reproduction
*   **Definition**: Observed by someone actively hostile to the project's hypothesis, actively trying to disprove or falsify the methodology.
*   **Evidence Level**: Very Strong.
*   **Target**: Minimum 3 hostile reviews confirming the phenomenon (even if they dispute the underlying cause).
*   **Status**: 0% Proven.

### Gate 4 — Community Confirmation
*   **Definition**: Observed repeatedly by unrelated groups, leading to third-party usage and independent citations.
*   **Evidence Level**: Highest practical level.
*   **Target**: 10+ independent reproductions.
*   **Status**: 0% Proven.
